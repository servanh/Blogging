About Me

This is what I do.

Chicana Mom was born in South Texas in the early 80s and is the daughter of Mexican immigrants. She was raised to hustle working in her dad’s arcade on the weekends in Mexico. Each Friday after school she would cross the border into Mexico then travel back on Sunday night to get ready for school the next day in South Texas. During her weekends in Mexico she also sold Clorox and diapers, the American kind, you know because everyone knows American cloro and pañales are the best. There were other times that her mother would have her sell chamoyadas with her crazy drunk uncle Vicente because he had suerte to attract business. The chamoyadas were conveniently located in an alley next to the arcade. So business was good sometimes and sometimes it wasn’t. She had a great time spending those weekends in Mexico because what other 9 year old doesn’t want to have unlimited credits to play Streetfigher all night long each Saturday after the store closed. When Chicana Mom turned 12, her dad decided to pack the family up and move to a small town in Oklahoma, which was very white and not brown at all. It got pretty lonely, as being the only Latina girl in your 6th grade class wasn’t the easiest. Chicana mom got through the awkward teenage years and always said she would leave Oklahoma but ended up staying after finding love, getting married and having a beautiful son where she couldn’t think of a better place to raise him. You see Chicana mom knows that us Latinos can fit anywhere and can do anything, even in Oklahoma.
